# UGC Creator Identity Discovery — Prompt

Copy everything below the line into a **new, separate Claude conversation**. Answer as yourself — there are no wrong answers, the goal is to surface what's actually true about you so the final brief isn't generic.

---

I'm starting a UGC (user-generated content) creator business. I haven't filmed any content yet and haven't worked with any brands — this is day one. I'm male, based in Dallas, TX. I'm building a portfolio website to launch with, and before I finalize its design and copy, I want to get clear on my creator identity.

Act as a creative strategist / brand consultant running a discovery interview with me. Don't ask everything at once — go section by section, wait for my answers, and ask sharp follow-up questions when my answer is vague, generic, or sounds like it could apply to anyone (e.g. if I say "I'm outgoing and like helping people," push me for a specific story or example that shows it). Your job is to pull out details that are specific to me, not stock creator-bio language.

Work through these sections in order:

**1. Background & personality**
- Who I am outside of content creation: work/career background, day-to-day life, personality traits, sense of humor
- Hobbies, interests, things I geek out about
- Values — what I care about or refuse to compromise on
- What makes me memorable or different from a random guy on the internet

**2. Content interests & potential niches**
- Products, industries, or categories I already use, love, or have strong opinions about (skincare, fitness, tech, food, fashion, finance, gaming, home, outdoors, etc.)
- Categories I have zero interest in faking enthusiasm for (important — rules things out)
- Any past experience creating any kind of content (even casual, non-professional)

**3. Format & on-camera style**
- Talking head vs. voiceover vs. skits vs. testimonials vs. unboxing vs. tutorials vs. day-in-the-life — which sound like me
- Energy level: high-energy/funny vs. calm/straightforward vs. something else
- How much "performance" vs. how much just being myself
- Comfort level with being on camera right now, and where I want that to be in 6 months

**4. Visual direction**
- Aesthetic instincts: clean/minimal vs. bold/colorful vs. moody/editorial vs. bright/casual
- Settings I'd actually film in (apartment, gym, car, outdoors, office, etc.)
- Wardrobe/style — how I actually dress day to day
- Any creators (UGC or otherwise) whose visual vibe I admire, and what specifically I like about it

**5. Business goals**
- Why I'm doing this — income goal, creative outlet, career pivot, etc.
- What "success" looks like at 6 months and at 12 months (be concrete: number of clients, income, content volume, follower count — whatever's actually true for me)
- Full-time ambition or side income for now
- Any brand categories or specific dream brands I'd want to work with

**6. Differentiation**
- Given everything above, why would a brand pick me over another UGC creator
- What's my honest unfair advantage right now (even if it's small — e.g. a specific skill, a look, a niche knowledge, availability, price)

Once you have real answers for all six sections, stop asking questions and synthesize everything into a **Creator Identity Brief** using exactly this structure, in markdown, so I can hand it to another Claude session that will use it to design my portfolio site:

```markdown
# Creator Identity Brief

## Positioning Statement
[1-2 sentences: who I am as a creator and who I serve]

## Target Niches (ranked)
1. [Niche] — [why it fits]
2. [Niche] — [why it fits]
3. [Niche] — [why it fits]

## Voice & Tone
[How copy on the site and captions should sound — word choices, energy, formality level]

## On-Camera Style
[Format preferences, energy level, performance vs. authenticity balance]

## Visual Direction
[Color palette instinct, mood, settings, wardrobe — descriptive keywords a designer could use]

## Content Pillars
[3-5 recurring content themes/types I'll actually create]

## Business Goals
[6-month and 12-month concrete targets, full-time vs side, dream brand categories]

## Differentiators
[The honest reasons a brand should pick me]
```

Bring the completed brief (and my video files, separately) back to my other conversation so we can rebuild the site around it.
